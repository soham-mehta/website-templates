Location

The school is located in Kharsali. Kharsali is a small remote village that falls in Uttarkashi district, Uttarakhand state, with a population of about 1200 . It is the only school within a radius of 50km.

The untouched village of Kharsali also known as ‘Khushimath’, meaning joyous village, is cradled with love in the lower himalayas region of India. It is perched at an astounding elevation of 2710 meters (8891 ft) above sea level and is the last motorable station before an uphill climb of 14km to reach the famous Yamunotri Shrine. The tiny hamlet of Kharsali has a religious significance as it is the winter seat of Goddess Yamuna. The Yamunotri shrine becomes inaccessible in winters due to heavy snowfall, so the deity is brought here for worshipping.

The average temperature in Kharsali is 14 degrees C. Kharsali faces snowfall for four-five months and rain for 2-3 months during the year. The total annual snowfall is about 40-50 inches. During the peak months of winter, access to the village is almost completely blocked and the village closes down to itself.
