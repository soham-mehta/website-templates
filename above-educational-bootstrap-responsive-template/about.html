About

Cause

We visited kharsali for the first time as part of a visit to the yamunotri temple. Here we noticed that major parts of the village were run down and some houses were abandonded, showings signs that people had migrated. When we asked the villagers about the reasons for the same, they cited that education for children and connectivity in this rapidly evolving world was a must and was not available to them. On further enquiry, we were told that a "school" existed in the form of a 12ft x 12ft room where one teacher taught 9 students on a small chalkboard. No books, no stationary , no tables, no classes, nothing you would see in a normal school. There were about 200 children in the village and 90 % of them had never been educated before. The handful of children who were being educated had to travel 1.5 hours to reach the nearest school. This inspired the thought of building a school for the village.


Process of building


Kharsali is a remote village and is one of the closest occupied areas before the Himalayan mountain chain starts in the north of India. Hence resources to build the school all had to be outsourced from Dehradun(180kms - 6 hour drive). An architect and contractor were hired from Delhi. The school was built over a span of 13 months and took over 15 visits( Maharashtra to Uttarakhand )from start to end.


Current Status

The school is a 3 floored building along with 7 classrooms, a small library and a computer lab equipped with 8 PCs. Currently the school has one section each for grades 1 through 7 and 182 students total. The school is well equipped with e-learning facilities, a library , and various other extracurricular activities such as :

  • Cricket
  • Carrom
  • Badminton
  • Basketball
  • Dance
    • Drama / Plays
    Prayer recital


Additional info

The school is recognised by the Uttarakhand government and is the only school within a radius of 50 km. It is one of the few schools in India above an altitude of 2500m.
Regular events are held in the school and students compete with each other in various dance, sports and cultural activity events. One of the biggest success of the school was the implementation of the e-learning facility and wi-fi connection. Students who had never seen a PC in their life are now able to operate basic applications like Word, Excel, Paint.
